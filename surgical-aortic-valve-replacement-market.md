<p><p>The Surgical Aortic Valve Replacement market encompasses various approaches, including the transfemoral, transapical, and others. The transfemoral approach involves accessing the heart through the femoral artery, offering a less invasive option for patients. The transapical approach requires an incision in the chest wall, allowing direct access to the heart, suitable for those unable to undergo transfemoral procedures. Other methods may include subclavian or direct aortic approaches. Each method is tailored to patient needs, anatomical considerations, and procedural effectiveness.</p></p>

<p><p>Rail battery systems are essential for energy storage and powering trains. Lead-acid batteries are traditional, cost-effective, and reliable but have lower energy density and shorter lifespans. Nickel-cadmium batteries offer better performance in extreme temperatures and longer cycle lives, though they come with higher costs and environmental concerns. Lithium-ion batteries are increasingly popular due to their high energy density, lightweight design, and longer lifespan, making them ideal for modern rail applications, enhancing efficiency and reducing operational costs.</p></p>

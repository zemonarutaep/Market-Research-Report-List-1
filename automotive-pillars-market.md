<p><ul><li>A-Pillar</li><li>B-Pillar</li><li>C-Pillar</li><li>D-Pillar</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Automotive pillars are structural components of a vehicle's frame, categorized into A, B, C, and D pillars, each serving unique functions. The A-pillar supports the windshield and influences aerodynamics; the B-pillar provides structural integrity and access to rear doors; the C-pillar reinforces the rear structure and enhances safety; and the D-pillar, found in larger vehicles like SUVs, adds stability and carries the rear roofline. The market for these pillars varies based on automotive design, safety regulations, and consumer preferences.</p></p>
